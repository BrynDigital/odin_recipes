Mushroom Tacos

This mushroom tacos recipe is quick to make and bursting with savory flavor, starring meaty portobellos and creamy refried beans!

This mushroom tacos recipe comes together in a flash: it should take you about 20 to 25 minutes to put together. The taco is a Mexican invention, likely from the Aztec times, and was first introduced in the US in 1905. We honor the great tradition of Mexican cuisine in this recipe! The only part that requires cooking is sautéing the portobello mushrooms with a few spices. Combine those with canned refried beans to add enough protein to keep these tacos filling. A Crowd-Pleasing Recipe Ree’s mushroom tacos are made extra special with the addition of spices such as cumin, paprika and chili powder. What’s more, this is one of Ree’s 16-minute meals, so it goes from pan to plate in very little time.

  • Prep Time: 45 mins
  • Cook Time: 1 hrs 15 mins
  • Additional Time: 10 mins
  • Total Time: 1 hrs 20 mins
  • Servings: 4

How to Make Tacos This mushroom tacos recipe is so simple to make and bursting with flavor! Here’s one note to keep in mind on serving size: The mushrooms shrink down in the pan. It looks like a lot at first, but as they cook off the water the mushrooms shrink in volume. Add just a handful of mushrooms per taco. It will still add massive flavor. Place them on a generous helping of refried beans. If you’re feeding very hungry eaters, make 1.5 times the mushrooms and double the beans. Or, you can accessorize with more hearty side dishes! See below. All you’ve got to do is sauté the mushrooms with spices, then make a quick slaw and top them with toppings. You don’t even need to make a sauce, though you can top with sour cream if you like. They’re juicy and delicious as is!

Step-By-Step

Here's a very brief overview of what you can expect when you make homemade Tacos:
  1. Sliced mixed mushrooms
  2. Garlic
  3. Yellow cherry tomatoes, quartered
  4. Street taco corn tortillas, for serving
  5. Hot Sauce
  6. Cream Sauce
  7. Jalapeño, seeded and diced small

Ingredients

  1. 1 pound sweet Italian sausage
  2. 1 generous cup chopped onions
  3. 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil, plus more for oiling the noodles
  4. 4 cloves garlic, minced
  5. 1/2 cup jarred pickled red onions
  6. 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  7. Juice of 1 lime

Directions

For the mushroom tacos: Char and warm the tortillas in a skillet for 20 to 30 seconds each in a dry pan. Wrap in a cloth to keep warm.

Heat the olive oil and butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the mushrooms and onions, then cook until the mushrooms have released their moisture and begun to brown, 7 to 8 minutes. Add the garlic, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, pepper and chile flakes. Stir and cook, an additional 2 to 3 minutes. Stir in the lime juice.

For the pickled onion pico de gallo: Combine the cherry tomatoes, pickled red onions, cilantro, cumin, salt, pepper, jalapeño and lime zest and juice. Toss to combine. Taste and adjust the seasonings.

Add the mushrooms and cook 5 to 6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened. Lower the heat to medium and stir in the additional 1 tablespoon olive oil, garlic, cumin seed, cumin, smoked paprika

Warm and char the tortillas by placing them on an open gas flame on medium for a few seconds per side, flipping with tongs, until they are slightly blackened and warm.
